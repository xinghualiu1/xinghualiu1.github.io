This page highlights selected funded research projects in shared mobility, transport governance, and sustainable urban systems.

Projects span large-scale mobility data analysis, transport resilience, environmental assessment, and policy-oriented urban research.

---

## Financing Clean Air: The Potential of Land Value Capture to Secure Sustainable Urban Development and Enhance Air Quality

**Period:** 2019-2022  
**Funders:** National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC); Joint Programming Initiative Urban Europe (JPI Urban Europe)  
**Partners:** University of Liverpool; Universite Paris-Est; Radboud University; Tongji University; Xi'an Jiaotong-Liverpool University

**Contribution**

Served as student lead for the China team. Coordinated international collaboration, delivered progress updates, and led the China-side empirical analysis linking urban development, environmental quality, and transport-related policy issues.

---

## Theoretical Research on the Modernization of Urban Traffic Governance

**Period:** 2019-2022  
**Funder:** National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC)

**Contribution**

Investigated transport resilience and behavioural responses during public-health emergencies using social-media and mobility-related data. Contributed to policy-oriented analyses on public attitudes, equity, and governance.

---

## Carbon Emissions Trading for Road Traffic: Mechanism Design under Government-Enterprise-Resident Co-governance

**Period:** 2018-2021  
**Funder:** National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC)

**Contribution**

Analysed shared-mobility travel behaviour and developed quantitative methods to estimate the environmental impacts of ride-splitting and other shared transport services.

---

## Big-Data-Driven Paradigm Shift of Management and Decision on Shared Mobility

**Period:** 2016-2019  
**Funder:** National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC)

**Contribution**

Developed trip-identification methods for ride-splitting based on large-scale mobility records and designed a data-driven framework to evaluate environmental and behavioural impacts of shared mobility services.
